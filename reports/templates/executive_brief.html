Executive Summary. This report presents a data-driven valuation and 6-year growth forecast for the global artificial intelligence industry across four distinct value-chain segments: AI Hardware (chips and specialized silicon), AI Infrastructure (cloud compute and data centres), AI Software & Platforms (foundation models and AI SaaS), and AI Adoption (enterprise deployment spend). The analysis combines econometric time-series modeling (ARIMA and Prophet) with machine-learning residual correction (LightGBM) and explicit structural-break modeling at the 2022 GenAI inflection point. Forecast confidence intervals reflect model uncertainty from expanding-window cross-validation across all four segments.

Data Sources & Methodology

Data Sources

Market size estimates are derived from three independent institutional data sources covering macro indicators, R&D expenditure, and public company financials. No single source provides a direct "AI market size" measure — the methodology uses proxy indicators (PCA composite index) scaled to a ${{ "%.0f"|format(anchor_total_usd) }}B industry consensus anchor at {{ anchor_year }}.

Methodology Overview

The forecasting methodology combines two modeling approaches blended via inverse-RMSE ensemble weighting:

  • Bottom-up proxy composite: PCA first principal component of 3–4 standardized proxy indicators per segment, fit on training data only (no look-ahead). Scaled to USD using value chain multipliers anchored at the {{ anchor_year }} consensus market estimate.
  • Statistical time series baseline: Per-segment model competition between ARIMA (pmdarima auto_arima with AICc) and Prophet (with explicit 2022 GenAI changepoint). Winner selected by expanding-window cross-validation MAPE.
  • ML residual correction: LightGBM trained on statistical model residuals using macro features, correcting for structured patterns the baseline cannot capture.

Confidence intervals are constructed from the ensemble's temporal cross-validation residual distribution, providing empirically-grounded uncertainty bounds rather than parametric assumptions.

Disclaimer & Notes

This report was generated from real-time API data (World Bank, OECD, LSEG) and hybrid econometric–ML models. Market size estimates represent model outputs and should be interpreted as indicative ranges, not definitive measurements. Confidence intervals reflect model uncertainty; additional uncertainty from data quality, structural breaks beyond {{ vintage }}, and unknown macro shocks is not captured in the CI bands.

Known Limitations

  • Private company exclusion: Pre-IPO AI companies (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic, Mistral) are not in the LSEG TRBC universe. Market size may be understated by 20–40% in software/adoption segments.
  • Geographic coverage: 19 economies covering ~85% of global AI activity. Emerging AI ecosystems (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Vietnam, Brazil) are not covered.
  • Segment overlap: GPU revenue may be double-counted across Hardware and Infrastructure segments. See config/industries/ai.yaml for documented overlap ranges.
  • Forecast horizon: Forecasts beyond 2027 carry materially higher uncertainty. Point estimates for 2028–2030 should be interpreted alongside the 95% CI band.

Methodology Deep Dive

Hybrid Model Architecture

The forecast pipeline uses a three-layer hybrid architecture:

  1. PCA Composite Index (Bottom-up layer): For each segment, 3–4 standardized proxy indicators (from World Bank, OECD, LSEG) are combined using Principal Component Analysis. The first principal component is the AI market activity index. Standardization is performed on training data only inside each CV fold to prevent look-ahead bias.
  2. Statistical Time-Series Baseline (Statistical layer): ARIMA (pmdarima auto_arima with AICc criterion) and Prophet (with explicit 2022 GenAI changepoint at scale 0.1) compete per segment using expanding-window temporal cross-validation. Winner is selected by out-of-sample MAPE. The winning model generates a forecast and residuals for 2010–{{ vintage }}.
  3. LightGBM Residual Correction (ML layer): A LightGBM model is trained on the statistical residuals using macro features (GDP growth, ICT exports, R&D intensity). The correction is additive: ensemble = stat_prediction + lgbm_weight × correction.

Ensemble Weighting Formula

The statistical and ML layers are blended using inverse-RMSE weights from cross-validation:

w_stat = (1/RMSE_stat) / (1/RMSE_stat + 1/RMSE_lgbm)
w_lgbm = (1/RMSE_lgbm) / (1/RMSE_stat + 1/RMSE_lgbm)
ensemble_forecast = w_stat × stat_forecast + w_lgbm × lgbm_correction

An epsilon guard (1×10⁻¹⁰) prevents division-by-zero for near-perfect in-sample fits.

Value Chain Multiplier Calibration

The PCA composite is a dimensionless index (zero-centered PCA scores). Conversion to USD uses:

multiplier_i = anchor_usd_i / index_i_at_{{ anchor_year }}
anchor_usd_i = ${{ "%.0f"|format(anchor_total_usd) }}B × segment_share_i

The ${{ "%.0f"|format(anchor_total_usd) }}B anchor ({{ anchor_year }}) is the median of three independent consensus estimates: McKinsey Global Institute ($185–200B), Statista ($207B), and Grand View Research ($196B). All within ±5%.

Structural Break Modeling (2022 GenAI Surge)

A three-step structural break analysis is applied per segment:

  1. CUSUM detection: Constant-only OLS residuals tested via CUSUM test (p < 0.05 threshold)
  2. Chow confirmation: F-statistic confirms statistical significance at the detected break year
  3. Markov Switching: Two-regime model with shared slope but regime-specific intercepts captures the gradual transition from pre-GenAI to post-GenAI growth trajectory

Minimum 20 observations required for Markov EM convergence. Fallback to dummy-variable OLS for shorter series.

Confidence Interval Construction

CIs are empirically constructed from the distribution of temporal CV forecast errors, not from parametric assumptions. This provides honest uncertainty bounds that capture actual out-of-sample performance across folds rather than asymptotic distributional assumptions that may not hold on 15-year annual panels.

Mathematical Appendix

AICc Small-Sample Correction

Standard AIC is modified by Akaike's small-sample correction (AICc) for annual panels where n < 30:

AIC = −2ℓ + 2k
AICc = AIC + 2k(k+1) / (n − k − 1)

where k is the number of estimated parameters and n is the number of observations. With n=15 and ARIMA(2,1,2) (k=6), the AICc penalty is 21 points larger than AIC — effectively ruling out higher-order models unless the fit improvement is substantial.

n ARIMA(1,1,1) k=4 ARIMA(2,1,2) k=6
15 +4.00 +21.00
20 +2.67 +9.33
50 +0.83 +1.94

Inverse-RMSE Ensemble Weights

Ensemble weights blend the statistical and ML model predictions:

w_i = (1/RMSE_i + ε) / Σ_j (1/RMSE_j + ε), ε = 1×10⁻¹⁰

The epsilon guard assigns near-maximum weight to any model with near-zero RMSE (perfect in-sample fit), while preventing division-by-zero. Weights are recomputed per segment from the corresponding CV RMSE values.

Additive Ensemble Blend

The final ensemble forecast is constructed additively:

ensemble(t) = w_stat × stat_pred(t) + w_lgbm × lgbm_correction(t)

where lgbm_correction is the LightGBM model's predicted value for the statistical residual at time t, not a full independent forecast. This architecture preserves the statistical model's extrapolation properties while correcting for structured macro-driven errors.

Confidence Interval Construction

Confidence intervals are derived empirically from temporal CV residuals. For each hold-out fold, forecast errors e(t) are collected. The CI at level α is:

CI_α(t) = [forecast(t) − q_{(1+α)/2} × σ_e, forecast(t) + q_{(1+α)/2} × σ_e]

where σ_e is the cross-validation residual standard deviation and q is the corresponding normal quantile. Separate LightGBM models are trained for lower and upper CI bounds to allow asymmetric interval widths reflecting nonlinear macro-feature interactions.

Chow Structural Break Test

For a break at observation t*, the Chow F-statistic is:

F = [(SSR_full − SSR_pre − SSR_post) / k] / [(SSR_pre + SSR_post) / (n − 2k)]

where SSR_full, SSR_pre, SSR_post are sums of squared residuals from OLS on the full, pre-break, and post-break samples respectively, k=2 (constant + trend). Under H₀ (no break), F ~ F(k, n-2k). Rejection at p < 0.05 confirms a statistically significant break at t*.

PCA Composite Construction

For segment i, let X be the (n × p) matrix of p standardized proxy indicators over n years.

X̃_ij = (X_ij − X̄_j^{train}) / σ_j^{train} [standardize on training fold only]
X̃ᵀX̃ = VΛVᵀ [eigendecomposition]
z_i = X̃_i · v₁ [first PC score = AI activity index]

Explained variance ratio EVR₁ = λ₁ / Σλ_j is logged per CV fold. EVR₁ > 0.5 is required for the PCA composite to carry sufficient information for meaningful market size estimation.

Known Limitations

  • Private company exclusion: Pre-IPO AI companies are not in the LSEG TRBC universe. Market size may be understated by 20–40% in software/adoption segments (PitchBook/CB Insights estimates).
  • Proxy measurement error: The PCA composite uses ICT-sector proxies as AI activity indicators. If the first PC captures general economic development rather than AI-specific dynamics, all estimates carry a systematic level bias.
  • Geographic coverage: 19 economies covering approximately 85% of global AI activity. Emerging AI ecosystems are not covered, introducing a 5–15% understatement growing to 10–20% by 2030.
  • Segment overlap: GPU revenue may be double-counted across Hardware and Infrastructure. Per-segment estimates should be interpreted with the documented overlap ranges in config/industries/ai.yaml.
  • Forecast horizon: Forecasts beyond 2027 carry materially higher uncertainty. 95% CI bands at 2030 span a wide range — this is the honest model output, not a limitation to minimize.
